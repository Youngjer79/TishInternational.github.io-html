A variety of better living solutions for seniors and those with

Tish International, a global supplier of exercise and rehabilitation products for seniors and those with limited mobility, was founded by the oldest Domino’s® Delivery Driver on the planet, 50-year fitness and conditioning veteran, and trainer to seniors and boomers, Danny Lucien “The Solution”. He, like you, suffered from the pain of being on his feet all day.

If our products can keep him going

delivering pizza after pizza,night after
night, hour after hour, mile after mile,
step after step...just imagine what
they can do for you

Now in his golden years, Danny defies age

having made tens of thousands of deliveries,walked thousands of miles, and bent and stooped hundreds
of thousands of times, all after the age of 60.Imagine
the wear and tear on his body,let alone on someone
half his age and in their prime.

One night, after a grueling, painful, 8-hour,20-delivery shift

Danny staggered into the gym, tired and sore. Simply by
accident, while doing a particular exercise, he stumbled
upon a miraculous, pain-relieving technique. Over the
course of the next three years, he spent hundreds of
hours testing and experimenting with a variety of pro-
ducts, methods, and equipment.

Today, Danny is proud to bring you

only the best and most effective he has found – only those that actually work – only those he has personally tested and used himself – only those that have been “Tish-tested” and deserving of his “Tish Seal of Approval.

OUR FOUNDERS

It all began in the dungeon- like basement

of a Massachusetts YMCA in the summer of 1969. It was there, using antiquated equipment, mixed with Olympic lifting and Powerlifting exercises, that a local junior golfer set out on his quest to hit the ball further. In just three short months of training, Danny added a jaw-dropping, eye-popping, 50-plus yards to his tee shots, and decreased his approach shots by an astonishing 3 clubs (what once took a 6-iron to reach the green, now only required a 9-iron).

If not the first, Danny was surely one of the first,

golfers to incorporate strength training into the game. Once considered taboo, and thought to cause a muscle-bound, swing-diminishing condition, he, once and for all, dismissed the age-old myth.

Today, many top amateurs, and most touring pros, “hit the gym”.

They, too, have come to know, what decades earlier Danny first discovered, that when it comes to distance and scoring, strength and fitness matter

In the years that followed,

Danny cut back his play, choosing instead to focus on bodybuilding and strength training. In 1981, he opened his first gym in Worcester, Massachusetts, and has remained active in the sport ever since.
